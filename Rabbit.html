Rabbit

A rabbit is a small, long-eared, herbivorous mammal known for its powerful hind legs and short tail, belonging to the family Leporidae. These social animals live in underground tunnels called warrens and are characterized by their constantly growing teeth, which are worn down by chewing fibrous plants like grass and hay. The ancestor of all domestic breeds is the European rabbit, which has been introduced to many parts of the world.
